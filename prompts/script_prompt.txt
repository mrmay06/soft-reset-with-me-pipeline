SCRIPT FRAMEWORK:

HOOK (0-3s) — scroll-stopper.
The hook must be instantly understandable in the first half-second.
Use plain, direct words before poetic language.
It must create one of these reactions: "wait, I do that", "that might be me", or "I need to know if this is true."

Use one of these formulas:
A. Specific scenario: "If you [specific emotional behavior], this is for you."
B. Counter-intuitive identity check: "You think it's [normal belief]. It might be [deeper truth]."
C. Uncomfortable truth: "[Painful observation most people won't say out loud.]"

Strong examples:
"You think it's intuition. It might be trauma."
"If one late reply ruins your mood, this is for you."
"You might be triggered, not intuitive."
"That panic might not be a red flag. It might be old pain."
"If silence makes you feel abandoned, listen to this."

Weak examples to avoid:
"Your nervous system remembers things your mind calls intuition."
"Healing starts when you listen to yourself."
"Sometimes love teaches us the hardest lessons."
Never start with "Today I want to talk about."

TENSION (3-10s):
Name the exact pain or situation.
Make the viewer feel seen. They should think, "how does this know me."

INSIGHT (10-35s):
Land the reframe or revelation.
Use 2-3 sharp points max. No rambling.
Make every sentence earn its place.

LOOPBACK (18-30s):
Land the emotional moment and connect back to the hook.
Leave them sitting with something.

CTA FIELDS (not spoken by the voiceover):
Line 1 engagement question or soft prompt, used for comments/metadata:
- lists ask for comments
- scenarios ask for saves
- hot takes ask for opinions
- truth drops/reframes ask for shares or saves
Line 2 like/save CTA, used for metadata/end-screen strategy:
"Save this for when you need it." OR "Share this with someone who needs to hear it."

WORD COUNT:
- 75 words maximum across hook + tension + insight + loopback
- Ideal length is 20-35 seconds
- Write for a slightly fast 160-170 words per minute pace

COPY RULES:
- Gender-neutral by default
- Direct address: "you"
- No emojis in spoken script
- No full therapy-script language like "validate your feelings"
- Never say: "you are enough", "love yourself first", "hi guys", "so basically"
- Never diagnose anyone as narcissist, avoidant, toxic, crazy, broken, or abusive
- Never teach manipulation, revenge, chasing, or how to make someone miss you
- If danger or abuse is implied, point to trusted support

WRITE LIKE THIS:

"That's not love. That's hope with someone else's face on it."
"Soft does not mean weak."
