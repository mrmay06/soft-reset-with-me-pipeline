WRITING RULES:

WORD COUNT: {target_words_min}-{target_words_max} spoken words. Every chapter must support the core claim.

NARRATIVE SPINE (mandatory):
This is not a floating essay. Build the video around one recurring lived scene the viewer can picture.
The scene can be fictional but must feel real: a phone in bed, an unanswered message, a kitchen at midnight, a quiet walk home, a paused conversation, a moment of waiting.
Return to this spine at least 3 times:
- in the hook
- near the emotional cost section
- in the ending
The viewer should feel like they are moving through a miniature emotional film, not listening to a lecture.

HOOK (first 20 seconds):
The first sentence must make the viewer think "wait, that's me" — not "interesting topic."
Name a specific behavior, moment, or physical sensation — not a broad theme.
Good: "You finally meet someone who texts back. Who doesn't disappear for three days. Who is just... steady. And somehow that steadiness makes you want to run."
Bad: "Today we're talking about why healthy relationships can feel uncomfortable."

BANNED LANGUAGE:
- Generic self-help: "love yourself", "communication is key", "healing takes time", "you are enough", "set boundaries", "do the work"
- Clinical: "pattern interrupt", "nervous system activation", "regulate", "dysregulate", "co-regulate", "validate your feelings", "trauma response", "window of tolerance"
- Preachy closings: any sentence that sounds like a motivational poster

RETENTION RE-HOOKS (mandatory):
Open chapters 2, 3, and 4 with a single sentence (under 10 words) that reopens the emotional loop or pivots the angle.
Use them to create emotional altitude changes every 60-90 seconds.
Good examples: "But here's what makes this harder." / "This is where it gets darker." / "Here's the part most people skip." / "But there is something underneath that."
Bad: Starting a chapter directly with explanation or information.

MID-VIDEO DIRECT ADDRESS (mandatory — exactly once):
Between chapters 2 and 3, insert ONE sentence of direct viewer address that arrests the drop. Format:
"If that's familiar — stay with me. Because what comes next is the part that actually explains it."
Or: "If you've felt that before, don't leave yet. This next part is for you specifically."
This is NOT part of the chapter voiceover. It is a single line inserted between chapters.

HUMAN PSYCHOLOGY LAYER (mandatory, but translate it):
Use the psych concept as the hidden engine, not as spoken textbook language.
You may use ideas like intermittent reinforcement, ambiguous loss, cognitive dissonance, sunk cost, self-concept drift, fantasy bonding, or avoidance of finality.
But do NOT say them clinically unless the wording is simple and lived-in.
Bad: "This is intermittent reinforcement caused by anxious attachment."
Good: "If they were cold all the time, you would leave faster. But they are warm just often enough to make leaving feel like giving up too soon."

ESCALATING COST (mandatory):
The middle must get heavier before it gets clearer.
Show what the pattern is quietly costing the viewer: self-trust, standards, emotional energy, ability to want clearly, or the definition of what they deserve.
Give the darkest insight its own breath. Do not rush past it.

MIRROR MOMENT (mandatory):
Include exactly one direct, uncomfortable sentence that makes the viewer feel gently caught.
Examples:
"Be honest. If they texted you right now, you would still answer fast."
"You are not just waiting for clarity. You are helping the fog stay useful."
It must be emotionally confronting, not cruel.

DECISION TOOL (mandatory):
Before the soft ending, give the viewer one concrete question, test, sentence, or framework they can use tonight.
It must be specific enough to act on, but still emotionally on-brand.
Good: "If nothing changes in the next three months, if it stays exactly this undefined and exactly this warm and exactly this inconclusive, would I still choose it?"
Bad: "Set boundaries." / "Choose yourself." / "Communicate your needs."

EMOTIONAL PRECISION:
Include 2-3 moments of precise emotional observation — a specific behavior, a thought, a physical sensation — that the viewer has never seen named exactly that way before.
Good: "You check your phone not because you want a message, but because you need to know the silence means nothing."
Bad: "You feel anxious when they don't respond quickly."

ONLY SOFT RESET LINE:
This is the most important field in the output. It is the single sentence the viewer would screenshot and send to a friend. It must feel like something no one has ever said exactly that way before. If it sounds like it could be a generic quote on Pinterest, it fails. Rewrite it until it is specific, sharp, and slightly uncomfortable. It belongs inside the script — ideally in the final chapter or the reframe.

ENDING:
End with a soft emotional reset, not a motivational speech. Bring back the narrative spine from the opening so the viewer feels the loop close.
The final line should feel like a quiet exhale — permission, not instruction. Not "you've got this." Not "you deserve better." Something true and small and specific.

ENGAGEMENT QUESTION:
Write one question the viewer would type in the comments at 2am. It must be confessional or polarizing — not a survey. BANNED WORDS in the question: subconsciously, validate, process, dysregulate, attachment style (as a label), journey, nervous system.
Good: "Have you ever pulled away from someone just because they were actually there?"
Bad: "Have you ever experienced hypervigilance in relationships due to your attachment style?"

CTA:
Short, 1 sentence. Must leverage the specific emotional state the viewer is in at the END of this video — not a generic "save this." If the video ends on grief, the CTA acknowledges grief. If it ends on a quiet realization, the CTA honors that. Example: "If you needed someone to name that — subscribe. There's more of this here."
